From Healthcare Systems to Frontend

Journey • Personal Story

For years I worked in hospital information systems and patient registration — inside complex workflows, long queues and very noisy environments. This experience made me care deeply about clarity, empathy and stability in every interface I touch.

In this post, I write about how that background pushed me towards frontend development, why I care about calm design and how I’m slowly building a new career step by step.

Designing Calm Interfaces

UI/UX • Principles

“Calm” is a word I use a lot. For me it means: enough spacing to breathe, typography that doesn’t shout, and flows that don’t force the user to think about the interface when they are already tired from life.

Here I collect small principles I follow when designing landing pages and portfolios: hierarchy, rhythm, and soft contrast instead of aggressive visuals.

Building a Portfolio for Global Work

Career • Global Talent

I’m slowly preparing for a future where I can work remotely for international clients and maybe move to the UK or Europe. My portfolio is not only a collection of projects, but also a narrative about who I am and what I care about.

In this post, I reflect on the kind of projects I’m investing in, how I want to use my blog as proof of my journey, and what “global, but calm” means for my career.
